Yvonne Ellen

Yvonne Ellen home-ware is a truly unique range of tableware designed to add charm and intrigue to any home.
Yvonne's strong passion for beautiful fine bone china alongside a love of quirky, decorative illustration combines to create the one of a kind ranges within Yvonne Ellen homeware.
Yvonne gives unloved and long forgotten vintage ceramics a new lease of life for contemporary tastes.
We believe British is best and that is why every piece is carefully and lovingly designed and made right here in Britain. We also pride ourselves on only offering the highest quality products with exceptional attention to detail and finish. We only sell beautiful things that we would absolutely love to own ourselves. We truly love what we do and we do hope it shows.
Working closely with Yvonne, I was brought in take the business to that next level. By intimating upon the business a scalability that has allowed us to boost sales ten-fold and build upon the brand itself.
We are currently stocked in Liberties of London and on the web – on Not On The High Street and on Etsy.com In 5 short months we have been featured in a wide range of publications including The Metro (Newspaper) and in Sunday editions of the TheTimes and DailyMail and in magazines like TimeOut, Home&Gardens, Absolute East, Home & Antiques and the London commuter magazine - Stylist. Built a very solid platform to build upon.

Box Sweet [Archived]

While working within a franchisee chain of Mr Simms – a chain of old fashioned sweetshops, I grew a little frustrated that we were bound by physical boundaries. It is a business that appeals most to tourists as it taps into a bygone era and of quintessential Britishness that is ultimately very marketable.
There was a preexisting small transactional website business - operating from the Uxbridge Mr Simms branch with BoxSweet branded packaging (using shop stock). Great product – very visually appealing - but a platform unbefitting of, the business wasn’t performing at required level.
After a disappointing Christmas (2013) I was brought in in January 2014 and was tasked with its turnaround. I stripped the business utterly, essentially starting anew. Identifying a lack of fundamental structure or framework.
The focus was to be on the website -www.boxsweet.london [archived] - which I rebuilt on the Shopify platform. Shopify (and all rivals for that matter) I found to be restrictive and incredibly frustrating.
This paired with the reluctant acceptance that the UK confectionery market is notoriously fierce. The product by its very nature is extremely price resistant. With the average sale at £3.47 (before carriage) – at the bottom end of the market - margins are extremely tight. The line between profit and loss is by a hairline. The UK marketplace is utterly saturated. By January 2015 it became apparent we could not make the sums add up.
A sobering experience. Nonetheless it was an invaluable crash-course in a plethora of areas; perhaps most importantly is that that having total control over ‘front of shop’ implementation is simply vital. This helped me considerably in my decision to really start to understand web development and coding and to therefore take a 10-week part-time course on Front-End-Web-Development.
